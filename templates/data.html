Data


The first table contains 31 years of data between 1987-2017. S&P 500 and CPI data were downloaded from DataHub.io as a csv file. Disaster data was extracted from Emergency Events Database (EM-DAT) as a csv file. Both datasets were restricted to 1987-2017 to allow comparable datapoints for the machine learning portion of our project. The data was cleaned using Pandas in Jupyter Notebook.


The second table has 2019 stock prices and S&P 500 data. A company was selected for each industry to represent that industry as a whole. S&P 500 prices were used to represent the entire stock market as a whole. This data was extracted from Yahoo Finance, and Macro Trends. The industry data includes:

  • Consumer Staples (Walmart)
  • Information Technology (Apple)
  • Financial (Ameriprise Financial)
  • Consumer Discretionary (Amazon)
  • Utilities (American Water Works)
  • Materials (The Mosaic Company)
  • Health Care (Eli Lilly & Co.)
  • Energy (Marathon Oil)
  • Telecommunication Services (T-Mobile)
  • Industrials (United Airlines)
  • Real Estate (Equinix)
